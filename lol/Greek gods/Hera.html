Hera, the Greek goddess of marriage, childbirth, and family, is known for her strong personality and frequent outbursts of jealousy and anger, particularly towards her husband Zeus and his lovers. Many stories highlight her powerful nature and her interventions in the lives of mortals and gods alike. She is often depicted as a protector of women and a powerful force to be reckoned with in Greek mythology.Hera was fiercely protective of her marriage and often punished Zeus's lovers and their children. Examples include her persecution of Io, a priestess who Zeus transformed into a cow, and her attempts to kill Heracles as a baby.
