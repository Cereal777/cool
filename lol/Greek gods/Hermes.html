Hermes, the Greek god of messages and more, is a figure of many stories. He's known for his cunning, quick wit, and role as a messenger, but also for his trickery and even a bit of mischievousness. Here are a few key stories:

Messenger of the Gods:

.

Hermes is the primary messenger for Zeus, often delivering messages and helping to resolve conflicts, as seen in the Iliad where he helps Priam seek Hector's body from Achilles. Io and Argus: .

Hermes, under Zeus's orders, must free Zeus's lover Io from the watchful monster Argus, who has many eyes. He puts Argus to sleep with his panpipes and then kills him. Odysseus's Journey: .

Hermes assists Odysseus on his journey, offering him the moly plant to protect him from Circe's magic. Hermes and the Giants: .

Hermes fights alongside the gods in the war against the Giants, killing Hippolytos. The Emerald Tablet of Hermes: .

This legendary text is attributed to Hermes Trismegistus, a figure combining aspects of Hermes and the Egyptian god Thoth, and is associated with wisdom and alchemical knowledge. Trickster and Thievish: .
